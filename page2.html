THE BENEFITS OF URBAN TREES:

The oxygen produced by trees is essential for human life because we require oxygen to survive.

Trees also help to purify the air by absorbing pollutants such as carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide.

Trees with hollow trunks, cavities, and large branches can provide excellent habitats for birds, bats, and other animals. Fruits, nuts, seeds, and leaves are a crucial source of food for many species of birds and mammals.

The shade provided by trees can be a welcome relief from the heat of the sun, especially in areas with high temperatures or intense sunlight.
